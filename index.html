About Me

From working with semiconductor testing machines, writing software, to making lots of commercial products, I have gathered many years of experience in full product development life cycle.

Developing a product is not as easy as it seems, from designing and selecting the right component at the right price and right time requires a lot of discipline, dedication and patience. One single two cents component shortage will lead to the delay of the project launch. Ten days of public holiday at the manufacturing plant will either caused months of delay or increase of production cost if the product is to be made elsewhere. Sometimes, even over engineered software tool used in the development will lead to failure of a whole project.

That is why I nitpick on every bits and bytes in my projects so that they will stand a fair chance of being a successful project. I also focus a lot on simplicity by making error-prone complex processes simple and practical as I believe in anything that can go wrong, will go wrong.

A designer knows he has achieved perfection not when there is nothing left to add, but when there is nothing left to take away.

Antoine de Saint-Exupéry
